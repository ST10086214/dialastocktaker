General Questions

Stocktaking is the process of counting and verifying the inventory that a business has on hand. It's important because it helps businesses maintain accurate inventory records, identify discrepancies, prevent theft and shrinkage, comply with accounting regulations, and make informed business decisions based on accurate inventory data.

The frequency of stocktaking depends on your business type, size, and inventory value. Most businesses conduct full stocktakes at least once a year, often at the end of the financial year. However, many businesses also implement cycle counting (counting a portion of inventory regularly) or conduct quarterly stocktakes to maintain more consistent inventory accuracy throughout the year.

Dial a Stocktaker provides services throughout South Africa, with teams based in major cities including Johannesburg, Cape Town, Durban, Pretoria, and Port Elizabeth. We also serve surrounding areas and can travel to more remote locations as needed.

Our Process

The duration of a stocktake depends on various factors, including the size of your business, the volume and variety of inventory, the organization of your stock, and the complexity of your inventory. A small retail store might take a few hours, while a large warehouse could take several days. During our initial consultation, we can provide a more accurate estimate based on your specific requirements.

While closing your business can make the stocktaking process more efficient and accurate, we understand that this isn't always possible. We offer flexible scheduling options, including after-hours and weekend stocktakes, to minimize disruption to your business operations. For businesses that need to remain open, we can implement sectional counting strategies to work around customer areas.

To ensure an efficient and accurate stocktake, we recommend taking the following steps before our team arrives:

  • Organize your inventory as much as possible
  • Ensure all items are properly labeled
  • Remove any outdated or damaged stock
  • Prepare your inventory records (if available)
  • Brief your staff about the stocktake process

Our team will provide a more detailed preparation checklist specific to your business before the scheduled stocktake.
